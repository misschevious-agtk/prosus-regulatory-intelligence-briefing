## Categories that trigger a finding

### AI-in-legal-tools vendor activity
- **New AI-in-legal-tools vendor launches**, major capability updates, integration announcements, **MCP / Skill compatibility releases**.
- Vendor **terms of service, DPA, IP indemnity, training-data carve-out, sub-processor list changes** for any tool in active use, pilot, or `[shortlist]`.
- **Vendor security incidents, breach notifications, deprecations, M&A events** affecting any tool in `our_stack`.

### Bar associations and ethics
- **ABA, state bar, SRA, NOvA, OAB, Bar Council of India, CCBE** opinions and guidance on AI in legal practice.

### Court rules and standing orders on AI in litigation
- **US federal and state court standing orders** on AI use, AI-generated filings, hallucination sanctions.
- **UK court guidance** on AI use; **SRA Risk Outlook** and warning notices on AI.

### E-discovery and e-disclosure
- Regulator guidance; **Sedona Conference outputs**; **cross-border discovery rule changes**.

### Benchmarking and surveys
- **CLOC, ACC, ILTA, IACCM** benchmark reports and survey data on legal-ops practices.
- **Thomson Reuters Institute, Altman Weil, Wolters Kluwer** legal-market reports.

### Outside-counsel pricing and AI billing disclosure
- **AI billing disclosure rules** and **law-firm AFA developments** — particularly when affecting outside counsel jurisdictions where Prosus has active panels.

### Legal-function metrics
- **KPI and metrics framework developments** for in-house legal functions.

### Knowledge management
- KM and precedent-capture vendor and methodology developments.

### Legal-tech vendor M&A and consolidation
- Thomson Reuters / Casetext, Reveal / Logikcull, Mitratech acquisitions, etc. — affecting our procurement choices.

### Agent platforms (Toqan-relevant)
- **AI agent platform developments** (Claude Agent SDK, OpenAI Agents SDK, MCP, Anthropic Agent Skills) where they affect Toqan's roadmap or any legal-AI vendor's roadmap.

### Benchmarks against external legal AI
- **In-house AI tool benchmarks against external legal-AI tools** (LegalBench updates, Vals AI evaluations, academic benchmarks for legal LLMs).

### Procurement and vendor management
- Procurement, vendor management, and panel-firm management benchmarks and best practices.

### Internal legal-tech adoption
- Adoption barriers and best practices (change management, training programs, hallucination tracking).

### Outside-counsel spend
- Hourly-rate and AFA market data; outside-counsel spend benchmarks.

### Cross-border data localisation and legal-function operations
- Data localisation rules where they affect legal-function operations (e-discovery, matter management hosting, vendor data residency).

### ESG / diversity / panel composition
- ESG, diversity, and panel-composition reporting frameworks affecting outside counsel relationships.

### Legal-ops headcount and structural benchmarks
- **CLOC State of Industry**, **ACC CLO Survey** — salary, headcount, structure.

- General "future of law" futurism without a specific tool, policy, or workflow implication.
- Legal-tech vendor news in product categories Prosus doesn't use and doesn't reasonably shortlist.
- Law-firm marketing about their own AI capabilities, **unless naming a Prosus-panel firm**.
- Bar-association opinions in jurisdictions where Prosus has no in-house legal function or active outside counsel.
