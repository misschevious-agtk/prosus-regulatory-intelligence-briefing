# Interrogation Checklist — Antitrust & Cartels

Run every incoming item against this checklist before deciding to escalate to a finding. The checklist is stable across deployments; the `needs.md` for this domain is what determines whether a "yes" answer is *material* to Prosus.

## Enforcement-and-effects questions

1. **New theory of harm we could be exposed to?** Does this signal a new theory of harm under Article 101 / Sherman §1 — information exchange, signalling, algorithmic collusion, hub-and-spoke through a common intermediary (cloud provider, payment processor, ad platform, AI vendor)?

2. **Trade association, SSO, or platform forum we participate in?** Does this involve a trade association, standard-setting body, joint venture, or platform Prosus or any OpCo participates in? Information-exchange enforcement is the highest-frequency Article 101 risk for digital platforms and the line on what counts as exchange has been moving.

3. **Vertical practice we use?** Does this touch a vertical practice we use — RPM, exclusivity, non-competes, MFN/parity clauses, territorial restrictions, online sales restrictions, dual-pricing, selective distribution criteria, long-term supply or exclusive-purchase agreements? Anne-Claire's Booking.com depth on MFN/parity should be reflected here: a "yes" on MFN is rarely Rank D for this monitor.

4. **Leniency calculus shift?** Does this shift the leniency calculus — is the agency rewarding self-reporting more or less, is the first-in / second-in / third-in differential moving, are markers being granted more or less easily, are settlement discounts narrowing or widening?

5. **Dawn-raid or document-preservation pattern?** Does this concern a dawn-raid pattern, document-preservation issue, or privilege ruling we should mirror in our protocols? Particularly relevant if it concerns cloud-stored, chat-app-stored, or ephemeral-messaging-stored documents.

6. **Safe harbour created or removed?** Does this create or remove a safe harbour we relied on — block exemption (VBER, R&D, specialisation), de minimis, ancillary restraints, single economic entity doctrine, or analogous?

## Policy and doctrinal questions

7. **Policy/doctrinal conversation shift?** Does this signal a shift in policy or doctrinal conversation that we should engage with proactively — an open consultation, a working paper circulating among NCAs, an agency speech advancing a new theory of information exchange or algorithmic coordination, even where no enforcement action has yet materialised? Per the persona inversion, this is not a fallback question; it can produce Rank A findings on its own.

8. **Economic-theory engagement?** What is the economic theory being applied (or implicitly being applied) — coordinated effects, unilateral effects, non-coordinated horizontal effects through information transparency, foreclosure of intra-brand competition? Is the agency using a different economic model than the one we assume in our compliance programme?

## Source and confidence calibration

9. **Source weighting.** Is the source a final decision, a statement of objections, a settlement, a judgment, an AG opinion, or commentary? Per `/output-schema.md`, weight accordingly. Agency speeches and working papers cap at `confidence: medium` unless a formal instrument follows. Academic working papers from recognised competition-policy authors count as primary signal for Monitor 02.

10. **Worst / best / most-likely reading.** Mandatory before filing. If the worst plausible reading does not differ meaningfully from the best plausible reading, the item is probably not material enough to file.

## Materiality test (gate)

If the answer to all of questions 1–8 is "no, this does not connect to any practice, deal, forum, or compliance obligation listed in `/antitrust-cartels/needs.md` within a 24-month horizon" — **discard, do not file**. Empty output is valid output.
