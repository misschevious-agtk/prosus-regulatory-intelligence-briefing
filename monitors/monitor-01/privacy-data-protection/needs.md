## Categories that trigger a finding

### EDPB outputs
- **EDPB binding decisions**, urgent Article 64(2) opinions, guidelines, and recommendations — especially on AI, ADM, recommender systems, children's data, dark patterns, lawful basis, cross-border transfers.

### National DPA decisions and enforcement
- **DPA decisions, fines, formal notices, and inspection findings** from the trusted-primary list — particularly **Italian Garante, CNIL, AEPD, Dutch AP, DPC, ANPD, ICO**, plus German federal and Länder DPAs.
- **DPA annual reports and published enforcement priorities** — read as forward-looking signals.

### Court rulings — privacy
- **CJEU and General Court rulings** on data protection. Grand Chamber rulings auto-Rank A.
- **National constitutional and supreme court rulings** on privacy that travel doctrinally: Bundesverfassungsgericht, Conseil d'État, UK Supreme Court, STF, Indian Supreme Court.

### US — state laws and FTC
- US state privacy law enactments, amendments, AG enforcement under **CCPA/CPRA, VCDPA, CPA, CTDPA, UCPA** and successors.
- **US FTC privacy and data-security cases and consent orders.**

### Brazil
- **ANPD enforcement**, regulatory sandboxes, public consultations, normative resolutions.

### India
- **DPDP Act implementation rules**, Data Protection Board decisions when stood up, MeitY notifications affecting personal data.

### China — Tencent and Qwen relevance
- **CAC cross-border data transfer decisions**, PIPL enforcement, DSL enforcement affecting Tencent or our `our_stack` vendors.

### EU-US Data Privacy Framework
- **DPF status:** Schrems-style legal challenges, adequacy reviews, EDPB opinions on DPF, Commerce Department decertifications.

### Cross-border transfer mechanisms
- **SCC updates** and EDPB guidance on **supplementary measures**.
- **Sub-processor list changes** for any `our_stack` vendor.
- **Vendor DPA template updates** and data-processing addendum amendments.

### Cookie-banner and consent management
- Enforcement patterns by member state.

### Dark patterns
- Enforcement — particularly **EDPB joint statements, CNIL, AEPD, Italian Garante**.

### Children's privacy
- **DPDP children's provisions enforcement**, UK Children's Code enforcement, California Age-Appropriate Design Code, COPPA reform proposals, **EDPB children's guidelines**.

### Health and sensitive-data enforcement
- Anything touching **e-pharmacy, telemedicine, fitness/wellness data, biometrics**.

### Breach disclosures
- In our sector or supply chain — **pattern detection rather than direct relevance**.

### Privacy-relevant AI rulings
- **Training data lawfulness, output as personal data, recommender system transparency obligations.**

### Workplace privacy and gig-economy data
- The **EU Platform Work Directive intersection with GDPR** is a live area.

### Cross-domain regulator cooperation
- DPA + competition regulator joint statements, DPA + AI office cooperation, DPA + consumer regulator cooperation.

## Out of scope

- Pure **AI policy without a personal-data nexus** → AI News persona.
- Pure **copyright cases** (including training-data copyright) → IP persona.
- **Privacy-enhancing-technology vendor announcements** without enforcement or regulatory hook → Legal Ops persona unless directly affecting `our_stack`.
- General cybersecurity news without a personal-data breach angle → out of scope (separate function).
- Workplace surveillance and employee monitoring stories where the company is not in scope.
- Academic privacy commentary without a regulatory or enforcement nexus.
- Cookie-banner litigation in jurisdictions outside `our_markets` unless precedent-setting.
- "Privacy paradox" or behavioural-economics privacy commentary.
- Marketing/martech industry commentary on privacy unless it surfaces a specific compliance shift.

## Standing questions — carry-with-you, re-asked against every plausible item

1. **"Does this decision or development change a lawful basis we currently rely on at any OpCo — particularly LI for advertising, profiling, or recommender training?"**
2. **"Does it change a data flow we operate — collection, processing, cross-border transfer, retention, or sharing across the Prosus ecosystem (the LCM training flywheel especially)?"**
3. **"Does it trigger or alter a notification obligation — breach, DPIA, ADM, children's processing — at any OpCo?"**
4. **"Is the EDPB or a Tier A DPA reading ADM, recommender systems, or AI training in a way that brings the LCM, Ailo, OLX Magic, Toqan, or PayU's credit decisioning into scope of Article 22 or LGPD Article 20?"**
5. **"What does this development say about regulator interpretation of consent quality, dark patterns, or anonymisation thresholds — and does our current practice survive that interpretation?"**
6. **"Is a vendor or processor in `our_stack` implicated — directly named, or by category (e.g. 'AI vendors processing EU personal data')?"**
7. **"What does this mean for Tencent's privacy posture and for Prosus's group-level disclosure obligations flowing from it?"**
8. **"What does this mean for cross-border data flows from EU/UK to non-adequacy jurisdictions, including the China-via-Qwen training question?"**
