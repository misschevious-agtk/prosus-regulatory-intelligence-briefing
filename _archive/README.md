## `superseded-pre-system-prompt/`

Two early-stage monitor specs drafted **before** their respective canonical SYSTEM-PROMPT files were supplied:

- `monitor-01-digital-regulatory/monitor.md` — Digital & Regulatory Monitor, single-persona, 9-section structure. Archived 12 May 2026.
- `monitor-02-eu-competition/monitor.md` — EU Competition Law Monitor, single-persona, 9-section structure. Archived 13 May 2026.

**Why archived (Monitor 01):** The operator-supplied SYSTEM-PROMPT (12 May 2026) moved Monitor 01 to a **multi-persona model** (one monitor = four domain personas — AI News, Privacy & Data Protection, IP, Legal Ops — sharing a schema and ranker). The old single-persona Digital & Regulatory M01 is incompatible with that architecture, and its scope is now genuinely covered by the multi-persona Monitor 01.

**Why archived (Monitor 02):** The operator-supplied SYSTEM-PROMPT for Monitor 02 (13 May 2026) and the subsequent Block 1 / Block 2 / Block 3 initialisation refined the Monitor 02 architecture from a single-persona spec (Pieter van den Berg as named counsel, EU-spine focus) to a **named-anchor-with-four-reading-modes structure** (Anne-Claire Hoyng as named anchor, four doctrinal domains: antitrust & cartels / abuse of dominance / merger control & FDI / digital markets, sector regulation & state aid). The substantive scope expanded beyond EU-spine to a global competition lens including US DOJ/FTC, CADE, CCI, SAMR, SACC alongside the European core.

**Why preserved:** The content (sources, personas, exposure analysis) is useful raw material if any of those domains is ever spun out as a standalone monitor in the future, and it preserves the architectural reasoning trail — specifically, the question of why competition law was ultimately split four ways rather than kept unified is answered in the original single-persona Monitor 02 draft in its original voice.

**Status:** Read-only reference. Do not edit. Do not promote back into `/monitors/` without explicit operator sign-off and a Change History entry.
