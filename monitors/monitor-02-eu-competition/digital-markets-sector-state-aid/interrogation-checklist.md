# Interrogation Checklist — Digital Markets, Sector Regulation & State Aid

Run every incoming item against this checklist before deciding to escalate to a finding. The checklist is stable across deployments; the `needs.md` for this domain is what determines whether a "yes" answer is *material* to Prosus.

A note on scope: this domain is the messiest of the four because the border between competition law and digital/sector regulation is being redrawn continuously. Items that look like pure sector regulation often have competition-law effects (DMA designations affecting platforms we depend on, Platform Work Directive transposition with no-poach implications, PSD3 / Instant Payments Regulation interacting with payments competition); items that look like pure competition often have sector-regulation effects (market investigations producing market-wide remedies, state aid affecting competitor cost structures). The checklist holds these together rather than splitting them.

## Enforcement-and-effects questions

1. **DMA scope.** Does this concern a gatekeeper designation, DMA obligation, or DMA enforcement action that affects platforms we use, platforms we compete with, or could foreshadow a designation of an OpCo? Prosus operating companies are not currently DMA gatekeepers but the size-based criteria and the qualitative-designation route are both moving.

2. **DSA obligations.** Does it concern a DSA obligation that interacts with our content, advertising, marketplace, or recommender-system activity? VLOP/VLOSE thresholds and member-state-level implementation patterns are both in scope. JET, OLX, and eMAG sit comfortably above DSA thresholds in their respective home markets and may approach VLOP status in some.

3. **Sector-specific competition rule in our industry.** Does it involve a sector-specific competition rule — telecoms access, energy unbundling, financial services (PSD3, Instant Payments Regulation, interchange-fee regulation, EBA/RBI/SAMA payment regulator activity), transport, healthcare/pharma (PharmEasy exposure on Indian e-pharmacy delivery and platform-favouring), travel sector (passenger rights, drip-pricing rules, dark-patterns enforcement)?

4. **State aid.** Does it touch state aid — new framework or revision (GBER amendment, temporary frameworks, IPCEI), recovery decision, formal investigation opening, FSR action that interacts with state aid analysis — affecting us, our competitors, or our suppliers? State-aid-to-competitors is a potential defensive complaint route worth tracking.

5. **Market investigation or sector inquiry with market-wide remedies.** Does it concern a market investigation or sector inquiry that could result in market-wide remedies (CMA-style market investigations, ACM market studies, AGCM sector inquiries, CADE market studies, CCI market studies)? These can produce conduct obligations or structural orders that bind all market participants regardless of dominance.

6. **Regulatory convergence or divergence between competition and digital/sector rules.** Does it signal convergence or divergence — DMA replacing Article 102 enforcement in scope; sector regulators using competition-style tools; competition authorities using sector-style ex-ante tools; UK CMA / DMU regime divergence from EU? The boundary question matters for which enforcement pathway is active in a given market.

7. **Platform Work Directive and no-poach competition angle.** Does it affect the transposition of the Platform Work Directive (deadline December 2026) in any of our markets, and specifically the no-poach / wage-fixing / algorithmic-management-as-conduct framing some agencies are developing? iFood, JET, Swiggy (stake), SkipTheDishes, and other courier-dispatch operations are all in scope here.

## Policy and doctrinal questions

8. **Policy/doctrinal conversation shift?** Does this signal a shift in policy or doctrinal conversation on DMA scope, DSA enforcement, sector competition rules, state aid framework, or the digital/competition border — open consultation, working paper, designation criteria revision, agency speech, OECD competition committee output? This is the highest-frequency Rank A trigger in this domain because the policy window is structurally faster-moving here than in the other three domains.

9. **Economic-theory engagement and ex-ante vs ex-post.** Is the proposed or applied rule ex-ante (imposing obligations regardless of conduct, DMA-style) or ex-post (assessing conduct against effects, Article 102-style)? Hybrid models (sector regulation with competition-law triggers) raise the most complex compliance design questions.

## Source and confidence calibration

10. **Source weighting.** Designations, formal decisions, court rulings, gazetted guidance — primary. Consultation papers and call-for-evidence documents — primary as policy signal even where not yet decisional. Agency speeches directional only, `confidence: medium` ceiling. Academic literature on DMA implementation, sector-regulation/competition-law interaction, and state aid is primary signal for Monitor 02.

11. **Worst / best / most-likely reading.** Mandatory before filing. In this domain, the worst reading often involves regulatory creep — a sector rule originally framed as narrow being read by enforcers as a hook into broader platform conduct.

## Materiality test (gate)

If the answer to all of questions 1–9 is "no, this does not connect to a platform, service, market, supplier, or counterparty in our footprint within a 24-month horizon" — **discard, do not file**. Items reading on adjacent sectors with portable doctrine are filed at `severity: 2` only, not discarded.
