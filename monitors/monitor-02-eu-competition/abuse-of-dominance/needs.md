## N1 — Categories of news that trigger a finding

- CJEU and General Court rulings under Article 102 TFEU — Grand Chamber and Chamber rulings auto-Rank A; AG opinions tracked for signal.
- DG COMP Article 102 statements of objections, infringement decisions, interim measures orders, commitments — particularly in digital, platform, and ecosystem markets.
- Tier A NCA Article 102-equivalent decisions and court appeals — ACM, CMA, BKartA, Autorité de la concurrence, AGCM, CNMC, CADE, CCI, China SAMR (Tencent-relevant), South Africa Competition Tribunal.
- Self-preferencing doctrine developments — Google Shopping progeny, Google Ad Tech progeny, marketplace own-brand prioritisation cases, recommender-system-as-abuse theories.
- Exploitative-T&Cs and excessive-pricing doctrine — Meta Platforms v BKartA progeny, national exploitative-conduct cases, AGCM hyperscaler T&Cs work.
- AEC test, intent, and effects-based-analysis developments — Intel renvoi, Qualcomm annulment, fidelity rebate cases, margin squeeze cases.
- Refusal-to-deal and essential-facilities doctrine — particularly in data-access and API-access contexts.
- Tying, bundling, and ecosystem-theory cases.
- Market-definition developments in food delivery, online classifieds, OTA, online retail, payments, e-pharmacy — every Tier A or Tier B precedent affects dominance characterisation.
- Tencent SAMR enforcement and abuse-of-dominance equivalents in China.
- Remedies precedent — behavioural vs structural, monitoring trustees, divestiture timelines, recommender-system transparency obligations as remedies.
- Private actions for damages under Article 102 — Skanska, Sumal, and progeny on group-liability theories.
- Academic literature on Article 102 doctrine — TILEC, Toulouse, CRESSE, CLEEN, Stigler Center, ProMarket commentary, SSRN antitrust working papers.
- Agency speeches and working papers on digital-platform dominance, ecosystem theories, recommender-system competition, and the Article 102 × DMA interface.
- OECD competition committee work on digital dominance, abuse, and ecosystem effects.
- Economist consultancy white papers from Oxera, Compass Lexecon, CRA, RBB, Frontier, E.CA on dominance, abuse, and effects analysis.

- Consumer-protection abuse claims without competition-law anchor.
- US Sherman §2 cases in markets and on theories without read-across to our Tier A jurisdictions.
- General "platform regulation" commentary without doctrinal anchor.
