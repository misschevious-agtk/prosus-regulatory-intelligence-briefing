## N1 — Categories of news that trigger a finding

- DG COMP merger clearances, prohibitions, phase II openings, commitment decisions, and Article 22 referral decisions.
- CJEU and General Court rulings on EUMR — particularly post-Illumina/Grail below-threshold and ecosystem-theory cases.
- Tier A NCA merger clearances, prohibitions, and call-in decisions — CMA (and CAT/CoA appeals), BKartA (including §39a), Autorité de la concurrence, CNMC, AGCM, CADE, CCI, ACM, South Africa Competition Commission, China SAMR (Tencent-relevant for M&A).
- Threshold changes — EUMR, national merger control regimes, transaction-value thresholds, foreign-investment thresholds.
- Below-threshold call-in patterns — Article 22, CMA discretion, BKartA, AGCM, CADE adverse-AC notifications.
- Killer-acquisition academic literature and agency adoption — Cunningham/Ederer/Ma progeny, DG COMP and CMA citations of academic work.
- Ecosystem and conglomerate theories of harm — agency decisions, court rulings, academic literature.
- Innovation theories of harm — particularly applied to AI, venture-portfolio adjacent deals.
- Remedies precedent — divestment timelines, monitoring trustees, upfront-buyer requirements, behavioural-remedy acceptance, carve-out methodology.
- Delivery Hero divestment commitment developments — modifications, alleged breaches, trustee reports, DG COMP correspondence patterns.
- Gun-jumping enforcement actions.
- FSR notifications, prohibitions, commitments, and framework amendments.
- FDI/NSI Act review developments — new sectors in scope, threshold amendments, prohibitions, conditions, framework expansion across Tier A jurisdictions.
- Sectoral merger guidance in food delivery, online classifieds, payments, OTA, e-pharmacy, e-tail.
- Comparable-deal precedent (clearances and prohibitions) in food delivery, classifieds, OTA, e-pharmacy, payments.
- Cross-border merger coordination patterns — joint reviews, parallel proceedings, divergent outcomes.
- Academic and consultancy work on merger control — TILEC, Toulouse, CRESSE, OECD competition committee, Oxera, Compass Lexecon, RBB white papers on merger effects.

## N2 — Out of scope

- Pure horizontal-restraint matters without merger nexus → `antitrust-cartels` reading-mode.
- Pure Article 102 dominance/abuse without merger spillover → `abuse-of-dominance` reading-mode.
- DMA gatekeeper designation and DMA enforcement without merger nexus → `digital-markets-sector-state-aid` reading-mode.
- Corporate-law and securities-law M&A developments without competition angle.
- Private-equity industry commentary without specific deal-doctrine anchor.
- Tax-driven structuring developments without competition implications.
