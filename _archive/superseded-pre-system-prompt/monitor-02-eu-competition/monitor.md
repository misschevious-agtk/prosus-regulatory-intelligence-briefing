## 2. Persona

- **Profession & seniority:** Senior Competition Counsel, in-house, Prosus Group (Amsterdam-based with regular Brussels travel). Leads the Group's response to EU competition matters and coordinates with portfolio-company GCs on DMA, DSA, EUMR, and Article 101/102 TFEU exposure.
- **Profile:** Owns Prosus's EU competition posture. Day-to-day work splits across (a) live cases and investigations touching portfolio companies (especially OLX in messaging/classifieds and Stack Overflow in AI-assistant interoperability), (b) DMA gatekeeper-status and compliance reads for third-party gatekeepers whose conduct affects Prosus markets, (c) merger-control filings for Group transactions, and (d) horizontal-precedent watching (CJEU and General Court rulings that re-shape platform competition law). Briefs the Group Legal & Public Policy lead, the CLO, and on landmark items the CEO/Board.
- **Reading context:** Morning, 08:00–09:00 CET, after the Brussels press cycle has fired. Will dip back at 15:00 CET when the General Court and CJEU typically hand down decisions. Often reading on mobile between meetings; long-form re-reads happen on Friday afternoons.
- **Decision authority:** Acts directly on items inside their remit; escalates landmark precedent or items with multi-portfolio impact to the Group Legal lead; engages external counsel autonomously up to a published mandate cap.

---

## 3. Purpose & Needs

- **Purpose:** Surface every EU competition law development — enforcement actions, court rulings, regulatory consultations, gatekeeper designations, and merger decisions — that affects Prosus, its portfolio companies, or sets precedent likely to reach them within 12 months.

- **Needs:**
  - Daily snapshot of what DG-COMP, the CJEU, the General Court, and the major national competition authorities (NCAs) did in the last 24 hours.
  - DMA tracker: gatekeeper designations, compliance reports, investigation openings, interim measures, and Article 6/7 case law as it forms.
  - DSA enforcement watch: especially where DSA and competition law intersect (e.g. self-preferencing complaints, dark-pattern enforcement against VLOPs).
  - Merger-control radar: EUMR Phase-I and Phase-II decisions touching digital sectors Prosus invests in (food delivery, classifieds, fintech, AI infrastructure).
  - Read-through items: rulings against third-party gatekeepers (Apple, Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon) that materially change the legal environment for OLX, Stack Overflow, or any Prosus messaging/classified product.
  - Article 101/102 horizontal precedent (cartel decisions, abuse-of-dominance rulings) where the legal test changes — even if the parties are unrelated.
  - State-aid items affecting digital infrastructure or AI investment that could shape Tencent / Prosus's investment thesis in EU markets.

- **Out of scope:**
  - Pure consumer-protection enforcement without a competition angle (handled by M01 Digital & Regulatory).
  - Privacy / GDPR enforcement absent a competition intersection (handled by M01).
  - Tax-state-aid as a fiscal matter (handled by M07 Tax Policy Monitor when live; until then surfaced only when a portfolio company is directly named).
  - Non-EU competition matters except where they form direct precedent on EU competition law (e.g. major US DOJ cases against gatekeepers where DG-COMP has publicly cited the case).
  - Internal Prosus M&A filings — those go to M06 M&A Antitrust Monitor when live; until then surfaced here only if the filing produces external precedent value (e.g. a novel EUMR theory of harm).

## 6. Filters

### User-set filters

- **Date range / recency window:** Default "last 24 hours" view, with a "last 7 days" panel for context items and flash events.
- **Jurisdictions / geographies:** EU-27, plus UK (retained for read-through and portfolio operations) and the EFTA Surveillance Authority. Outside-EU items only when they materially change EU precedent (e.g. US DOJ Google AdTech ruling cited by DG-COMP).
- **Prosus Relevance Filter:** **Tight.** Surface only items where (a) a Prosus entity or portfolio company is directly named or affected, (b) a third-party gatekeeper action materially alters the competitive landscape for OLX, Stack Overflow, iFood, or any Prosus messaging/classified/AI-adjacent product within 12 months, or (c) the item creates EU competition law precedent at the CJEU/General Court level that would apply to Prosus.
- **Topic emphasis:** DMA enforcement and CJEU/General Court rulings weighted heavier than NCA-only matters through 2026. EUMR digital-sector decisions weighted heavier than non-digital mergers regardless of value.
- **Length cap per briefing:** 6–8 items per day (jurisdiction-specific monitor default per `strategy.md`).

### Locked filters

**System-wide locks** _(inherited from `strategy.md` — never edited here):_

- No paywalled-only content without a self-authored summary
- No items older than 30 days unless flagged "historical context"
- No items sourced from blocked sources (Section 4)
- No PII leakage from sources
- No paid-promotion content treated as primary
- No speculative legal advice in summaries
- No items that violate Prosus external communications policy
- No silent removal of Tier-1 sources

**Monitor-specific locks:**

- **DMA and DSA enforcement items always surface** while those regimes are in force, regardless of the user-set topic emphasis. These are the spine instruments of EU digital competition.
- **CJEU and General Court judgements involving Articles 101, 102, 106 TFEU, or any provision of the DMA always surface** within 24 hours of publication, even if the user-set window is narrower.
- **Statements of Objections and interim-measures decisions issued by DG-COMP always surface** within 24 hours of publication.
- **Any item where Prosus or a portfolio company is directly named** by DG-COMP, an NCA, the CJEU, or the General Court always surfaces — even if the user-set jurisdiction filter would otherwise exclude it.
- **Court documents in proceedings to which Prosus or a portfolio company is a party always surface** within the published-document window.
- **Never drop the DMA case register, the DSA enforcement page, or the DG-COMP press corner from primary scope** while the EU competition regime is in force.
- **Merger-control items where any portfolio company is a notifying party, an interested third party, or a target always surface** even if the EUMR theory is routine.
- **Cross-references from M01 are always honoured** during calibration mode (per `cold-start-protocol.md`). After Day 14 calibration, this monitor may claim items where it is the most-specific monitor per `strategy.md` Section: Cross-monitor deduplication.

## 7. Output Format

### Briefing-level wrapper

- **Top of briefing:** Date · Monitor name · Item count · Run timestamp · "Brussels takeaway" — 2–3 sentence synthesis of what today is really about (e.g. "DG-COMP signalled it will treat AI-assistant gatekeeping under DMA Article 6(7) rather than wait for AI Act enforcement; expect formal proceedings against a second gatekeeper within 60 days.")
- **Item ordering:** Most need-relevant first. Tie-breakers, in order: (a) items directly naming Prosus or a portfolio company; (b) DMA/DSA enforcement; (c) CJEU/General Court rulings; (d) DG-COMP/NCA decisions; (e) commentary.
- **Length cap:** 6–8 items per day.
- **Tone:** Tight, factual, written for a competition counsel — assume reader knows Article 101, Article 102, the DMA structure, and EUMR thresholds. No glossary tone. "Why it matters" can be two sentences if a multi-step legal read-through is needed; otherwise one.
- **Footer:** Pipeline stats — sources swept, items considered, items selected, items filtered out, Tier-1 vs Tier-2 ratio.

### Per-item structure

Each card:

- **Headline** — case name + procedural posture preferred (e.g. "Case T-XXX/26 OLX Group v. Commission — application registered, General Court"). Source title or tight rewrite as fallback. ≤120 chars.
- **One-sentence summary** — what happened. Includes case number, parties, and procedural step.
- **Why it matters for Prosus** — explicit tie to one of the Section 3 needs. Names the affected portfolio company or the precedent vector.
- **Legal hook** — the specific legal provision in play (e.g. "DMA Art. 6(7)", "TFEU Art. 102", "EUMR Art. 8(1)"). Mandatory.
- **Source** — primary citation (Tier 1) plus a secondary if used for framing.
- **Date** — publication date.
- **Jurisdiction** — EU-level / national NCA / UK / EFTA.
- **Risk band** — low / medium / high. High = active legal-team attention this week or directly names a portfolio company; medium = monitor and prep; low = situational awareness.
- **Portfolio exposure** — affected Prosus entity or "Group" if portfolio-wide.
- **Owner** — internal Prosus owner if action is required.
- **Status** — new / triaged / in progress / awaiting response / closed.
- **Next action** — one sentence, only if action_required = true.
- **Deadline** — date, only if applicable (hearing dates, response deadlines, comment-window closes).
- **Estimated impact** — one short phrase (e.g. "Precedent — multi-portfolio", "Filing cost ~€400k", "OLX read-through, 12-mo horizon").

### Card actions (must be present on every item)

1. **Copy** — copy summary + source + legal hook to clipboard.
2. **Summarise** — expand into a longer brief, on demand. Brief must include procedural posture, legal test applied, and read-through to portfolio.
3. **Share** — push to Slack / Teams / email. Default Slack channel: `#eu-comp-radar`.
4. **Open source** — open the underlying source URL in a new tab.

### Pinning (Watchlist)

- Star icon on every card. Watchlist groups items by case where possible (one ongoing case = one Watchlist entry threaded with updates).

## 8. Feedback & Learning Log

### Feedback mechanism

- 👍 / 👎 per item directly in the dashboard.
- Free-text comment field per item.
- Weekly "what did we miss?" prompt every Friday at 17:30 CET, alongside the Friday wrap.

### Agent rules

- One 👎 is a signal, not a verdict. Log it; act on patterns of three or more comparable signals inside 14 days.
- Locked filters in Section 6 are never modified by feedback.
- Source removal requires owner sign-off and a Section 9 row. De-prioritisation does not.
- Positive signal acts faster: two 👍 on a previously-low-weight source triggers a weight bump within 7 days.
- **NCA-specific rule:** if a national NCA's items get three or more 👎 inside 30 days, the agent does NOT drop the NCA — it asks the owner whether to retier from primary to secondary. Pre-empting an NCA is not the agent's call.
- **Calibration rule (Days 1–14):** all source-weight changes are surfaced to the owner for approval, not applied autonomously.
