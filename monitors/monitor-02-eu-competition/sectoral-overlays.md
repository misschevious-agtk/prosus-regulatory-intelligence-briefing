## 1. Financial services — payments, e-money, fintech, lending

**Materially applicable across the group; Monitor 02 attention HIGH.**

Prosus exposure: **PayU** (India under RBI, GPO partially divested to Rapyd March 2025, PayU Turkey, PayU Czech Republic, PayU Romania), **iyzico** (Turkey under BDDK), **Remitly stake** (US).

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **EU PSD3** and the forthcoming Payment Services Regulation — competition-by-design provisions on access to accounts, instant payments, interchange.
- **EU Instant Payments Regulation** — mandatory instant payments at non-discriminatory pricing; competition-conduct rule for payment institutions.
- **Interchange Fee Regulation (IFR)** — already in force; periodic reviews track for tightening.
- **PSD2 access-to-accounts rules** — competition-mandated open access.
- **RBI digital lending guidelines** — India competition × consumer credit overlay affecting PayU NBFC activities.
- **BCB (Brazil) open finance** — competition-by-design framework affecting PayU and any future Prosus payments entrant.
- **EBA AI principles**, **MAS FEAT principles** — sector-AI overlay with competition implications for model-risk and customer-treatment.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Any sector-specific competition rule affecting payments (PSD3 access, interchange revision, Instant Payments enforcement) is Rank B by default per `ranking-criteria.md` Rank B "guidance_published AND jurisdiction ∈ our_markets". Becomes Rank A under Rule 18 (DMA / DSA designation) where the rule interacts with DMA-designated gatekeeper conduct, or under the standard severity escalation.

## 2. Digital platforms — DMA / DSA scope and adjacent

**Heavily applicable; Monitor 02 attention HIGHEST.**

Prosus exposure: **OLX Group**, **eMAG**, **JET (Lieferando, Just Eat, Pyszne.pl, etc.)**, **Despegar**, **Swiggy (stake)**, plus all consumer-facing platforms. JET, OLX Group, eMAG sit comfortably above DSA online-platform thresholds in their respective home markets and may approach VLOP/VLOSE status in some member states.

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **DMA gatekeeper designation pattern** — track for designation of gatekeepers Prosus depends on (Google, Apple, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, ByteDance, Booking Holdings) AND for designation threshold revisions that could bring a Prosus OpC closer to scope.
- **DSA VLOP/VLOSE designation pattern** — particularly for OLX, eMAG, JET in member states.
- **German GWB §19a designation** — separate from DMA, applies to platforms with "paramount cross-market significance."
- **UK CMA SMS designation under the DMU/SMS regime** — UK-specific equivalent.
- **National platform regulation** — emerging member-state rules on food delivery, classifieds, OTA. Particularly Brazil and India digital-markets proposals.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Rule 18 (DMA / DSA designation auto-rank, Rank A regardless of severity) directly applies. Any rule change to designation thresholds, including extensions of §19a or SMS regimes, is Rank A.

## 3. Gig economy — Platform Work Directive and no-poach competition angle

**Operationally live; Monitor 02 attention HIGH.**

Prosus exposure: **iFood couriers**, **JET couriers (Lieferando, Just Eat across markets)**, **SkipTheDishes couriers**, **Swiggy (stake) couriers**, **PharmEasy delivery network**.

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **EU Platform Work Directive (transposition deadline December 2026)** — algorithmic-management provisions read partly as competition-conduct rules. The no-poach competition angle (whether competing food-delivery platforms engaging in wage-fixing or no-poach agreements for couriers constitutes Article 101 conduct) is a developing doctrine the persona reads closely.
- **National transposition patterns** — particularly Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Spain, Poland.
- **CADE Brazilian gig-economy attention** — emerging.
- **CCI Indian gig-platform attention** — emerging.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Treated as `sector-regulation` axis findings under `output-schema.md`. Rank B by default; Rank A when a specific enforcement action against a competitor or near-competitor on no-poach or algorithmic-management-as-conduct grounds is filed.

## 4. Food delivery sector regulation

**Sector-specific; Monitor 02 attention HIGH.**

Prosus exposure: **iFood Brazil** (CADE TCC live), **JET across multiple jurisdictions**, **Swiggy (stake)**, **PharmEasy delivery component**, **Delivery Hero stake (divestment obligation under EUMR commitment)**.

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **Brazilian CADE food-delivery enforcement** — iFood TCC monitoring under Rule 11 (Rank A regardless of severity for any CADE communication).
- **National food-delivery sectoral codes** — emerging in multiple jurisdictions on commission rates, restaurant transparency, courier rights.
- **Restaurant-side parity-clause enforcement** — read against Anne-Claire's Booking parity-clause depth.
- **EU Platform-to-Business Regulation (P2B)** — affects food-delivery platform-restaurant relationships.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Rule 11 (iFood/CADE TCC monitoring) and Rule 17 (comparable-deal precedent in food delivery) both directly apply. Any food-delivery competition development in a Tier A jurisdiction is automatically Rank A.

## 5. E-pharmacy — Indian regulation × competition

**India-specific; Monitor 02 attention MEDIUM-HIGH.**

Prosus exposure: **PharmEasy** — Indian e-pharmacy is increasingly competition-scrutinised, particularly around delivery exclusivities, deep-discount practices, and platform-favouring of own-brand SKUs.

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **CCI investigation patterns** in e-pharmacy.
- **Indian Ministry of Health / CDSCO** rules affecting e-pharmacy delivery economics.
- **State-level e-pharmacy regulation** in India.
- **ANVISA Brazil** for any cross-jurisdictional e-pharmacy precedent.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Standard Rank A/B per `ranking-criteria.md`; no specific rule promotes e-pharmacy automatically. Watched closely for novel theories of harm (deep-discount predation, platform-favouring as Article 102-equivalent).

## 6. OTA / travel — consumer protection × competition

**Latam-focused; Monitor 02 attention MEDIUM.**

Prosus exposure: **Despegar/Decolar** (LatAm OTA).

**Competition-lens overlays to track:**

- **EU travel-sector consumer-protection developments** with competition-by-design provisions (drip pricing, dark patterns, personalisation).
- **Latam consumer-protection regulators** that increasingly use competition-adjacent tools — PROCON Brazil, DNDC Argentina, PROFECO Mexico, SIC Colombia.
- **Aviation-regulator competition action** affecting OTA-airline relationships — ANAC Brazil, ANAC Argentina, IATA-level pricing rules.
- **Personalised-pricing doctrine** — particularly the academic literature on personalised pricing as a competition issue.

**Monitor 02 ranking interaction:** Read against Anne-Claire's Booking parity-clause depth — every parity-clause development in any travel-sector regulator carries depth no other in-house counsel has. Rule 10 (MFN/parity doctrine auto-rank) applies whenever a Tier A ruling shifts MFN doctrine in travel.
