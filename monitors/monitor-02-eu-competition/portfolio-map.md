## Plausibly-dominant map — by product/geographic market

> **The "plausibly dominant" framing is deliberate.** Does not volunteer dominance concessions but plans assuming dominance characterisation could be reached. The bar for "plausibly dominant" is: an agency could establish dominance on a defensibly narrow market definition. **Anne-Claire's redline on this is the most consequential single redline in the whole monitor.**

**Plausibly dominant or near-dominant:**

- **iFood** — Brazilian online food delivery (the dominant national player; the 2023 CADE TCC on exclusivity addressed exactly this).
- **OLX Brasil** — Brazilian general online classifieds.
- **OLX Polska** — Polish general online classifieds.
- **Storia** — Romanian real-estate classifieds.
- **Imovirtual** — Portuguese real-estate classifieds.
- **Property24** — South African real-estate classifieds.
- **eMAG** — Romanian online retail and marketplace.
- **Thuisbezorgd (JET NL)** — Dutch online food delivery (legacy market leader; contested by Uber Eats).
- **Lieferando (JET DE)** — German online food delivery (market-leading; contested by Wolt and others).
- **Pyszne.pl (JET PL)** — Polish online food delivery (contested by Glovo and Bolt; "plausibly dominant" reading rather than dominant).
- **iyzico** — Turkish payments.
- **Decolar (Despegar)** — selected Latam OTA markets (national-market analysis matters; not group-wide dominance).
- **Swiggy (stake)** — Indian food delivery (duopoly with Zomato; near-dominant in duopoly framing).

**Material but not dominant:**

- **PayU** in larger payment-processing markets (position varies by jurisdiction; not dominant in any single market).
- **JET UK (Just Eat)** — three-way fight with Deliveroo and Uber Eats; dominance would be hard to argue.
- **Stack Overflow** — dominant in product category but the product category is itself contested by AI assistants.

**Not dominant:**

- All venture-portfolio companies; JET in markets it exited; eMAG outside Romania; everything else.
