By Patricia Figueiredo ( April 27, 2026, 16:17 GMT | Comment) -- The UK's chief privacy regulator said on Saturday that he was stepping back amid an HR investigation, but it emerged almost immediately that John Edwards actually stepped back two months ago. Legal experts say the belated disclosure raises questions about transparency and governance that could resonate for tech and other companies in affecting the ICO's ability to make decisions on its caseload and to set policy direction. The sudden disclosure by John Edwards, the UK's chief privacy regulator, that he had voluntarily stepped back from his duties creates a troubling headache for his office: a transparency problem of its own making....
