## Categories that trigger a finding

### EU AI Act and Member-state implementation
- EU AI Act **Codes of Practice publications and revisions** (GPAI Code, forthcoming high-risk codes).
- EU AI Act **implementing regulations, delegated acts, Annex III updates**.
- **Harmonised standards** (CEN-CENELEC JTC 21 deliverables).
- **Member-state AI Act market surveillance authority** designations, competence allocations, and enforcement openings — especially **Netherlands, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Ireland**.
- **EU AI Office** guidance, model evaluation methodology publications, and any communication on **GPAI threshold crossings**.

### US — IP and AI policy
- **US Copyright Office** guidance, registration decisions, and policy reports on AI training data or output protectability.
- **USPTO** inventorship guidance and post-grant proceedings touching AI.

### Frontier model releases
- Releases by any vendor in `our_stack`: **Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS/Amazon, Alibaba/Qwen**.
- Plus **DeepSeek, Mistral, Meta Llama, Google Gemini**.
- Any new entrant **crossing the GPAI compute threshold**.

### Vendor terms and contracts
- **ToS, pricing, indemnity, training-data, content-licensing, and data-processing-addendum changes** for any `our_stack` vendor.

### China — Tencent and Qwen relevance
- **CAC enforcement or guidance** affecting Tencent or Tencent's Hunyuan.
- **Chinese AI service-registration regime** decisions.
- **US/EU export-control developments** affecting Qwen, DeepSeek, or other Chinese-origin models in `our_stack`.

### Court rulings on AI liability and IP
- **N.D. Cal., S.D.N.Y., D. Del., CJEU, General Court, UK High Court (Chancery), Delhi High Court, Bombay High Court, Brazilian STJ/STF** on AI deployer or developer liability, training data, output ownership, or recommender system harms.

### Brazil — ANPD on AI
- **ANPD AI consultations, regulatory sandbox decisions, and enforcement actions** touching iFood, OLX Brasil, Despegar, or AI-in-marketplace generally.

### DPA decisions on AI under GDPR
- DPA decisions on AI under GDPR (Article 5, 6, 9, 22 grounds) where the decision **sets transferable doctrine** — particularly Italian Garante, French CNIL, Dutch AP, Spanish AEPD, Irish DPC.

### AI Action Plans
- **ICO, CNIL, AEPD, ANPD** AI Action Plan publications and updates.

### EDPB on AI × GDPR
- EDPB **Article 64(2) urgent binding decisions** on AI and ADM.
- EDPB guidelines on **AI Act × GDPR overlap**.

### NIST and ISO/IEC frameworks
- **NIST AI RMF** updates, crosswalks, profile releases (especially **generative AI profile updates**).
- **ISO/IEC 42001** certification developments and audit precedents.

### US state AI law
- US state AI law enactments, amendments, AG enforcement: **California TFAIA, Colorado AI Act, Texas RAIGA, Illinois HB 3773, NYC AEDT**, and successors.

### UK
- **UK ICO AI audits and enforcement**.
- **UK CMA AI Foundation Models work product** (track despite competition framing).

### Insurance market signals on AI
- **AI Security Rider terms, premium changes, coverage exclusions, declination patterns.**

### Bar association ethics on AI in legal practice
- Bar association ethics rulings on AI in legal practice in **NL, UK, US states, Brazil, India** — Rank B by default per Rule 5.

### Sectoral AI guidance — financial services, employment, children
- **Financial services:** EBA, RBI, BCB, OCC, MAS.
- **Employment:** EU Platform Work Directive implementation.
- **Children:** EU age-assurance, UK Children's Code, COPPA reform.
- **All Rank A when affecting a Prosus operating company per Rule 7 (sectoral-overlay upgrade).**

## Out of scope

- Pure privacy enforcement **without an AI angle** → Privacy persona.
- Pure copyright cases **without AI training/output** → IP persona.
- **Legal-tooling vendor news** (Harvey, Robin AI, Spellbook, Lexis+ AI, Westlaw Edge AI, CoCounsel) → Legal Ops persona.
- Pure capability benchmarks **without a product, contract, or legal hook**.
- General "future of AI" commentary, futurist takes, economy-wide productivity analyses.
- AI ethics commentary from academic ethicists **without a regulatory or enforcement nexus**.
- Open-source model release announcements **with no impact on `our_stack` vendors or no GPAI threshold relevance**.
- Investor commentary on AI companies' valuations **unless the commentary references a regulatory or compliance development**.
- AI-in-warfare / lethal-autonomous-weapons commentary (unless a Prosus venture portfolio company is somehow named — vanishingly unlikely).
