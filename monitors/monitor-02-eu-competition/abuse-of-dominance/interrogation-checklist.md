# Interrogation Checklist — Abuse of Dominance & Unilateral Conduct

Run every incoming item against this checklist before deciding to escalate to a finding. The checklist is stable across deployments; the `needs.md` for this domain is what determines whether a "yes" answer is *material* to Prosus.

A note on the gate: Article 102 / Sherman §2 doctrine is "live" only where Prosus or an OpCo is plausibly dominant in the relevant market. The dominance map in Block 1 (currently in redline) is what determines this. For OpCos plausibly dominant — iFood Brazil, OLX Brasil/Poland, Storia/Imovirtual/Property24, eMAG Romania, JET in selected markets, Despegar in selected Latam markets, iyzico in Turkey — Article 102 doctrine reads as live exposure. For the rest, it reads as watching-brief precedent only.

## Enforcement-and-effects questions

1. **Dominance definition shift in adjacent markets?** Does this change how dominance is being defined or measured in markets adjacent to ours — market definition methodology, share thresholds, the role of contestability, multi-homing, data-network effects, ecosystem theories of dominance, intermediation power?

2. **New or extended theory of abuse?** Does it introduce or extend a theory of abuse we could be vulnerable to — self-preferencing (Google Shopping line), tying (Microsoft, Google Android line), refusal to deal / refusal to supply (Bronner, Slovak Telekom line), margin squeeze (Telefónica, Deutsche Telekom line), predation (AKZO, France Télécom, Wanadoo line), excessive pricing (Aspen Pharmacare line), exploitative T&Cs (Facebook/BKartA line), discriminatory pricing, loyalty rebates, exclusivity rebates (Intel line)?

3. **Digital or data-driven market analogue?** Does it concern conduct in a digital or data-driven market analogous to ours — two-sided platforms, data-as-input, algorithmic decision-making, ecosystem foreclosure, single-homing vs multi-homing dynamics, attention markets?

4. **Effects vs form, AEC test, role of intent?** Does it touch on the effects-based versus form-based analysis distinction, the as-efficient-competitor test (and recent court treatment of it post-*Intel* renvoi), or the role of subjective intent in abuse assessment?

5. **Remedies precedent.** Does it affect remedies precedent — behavioural vs structural, interim measures, monitoring trustees, divestiture timelines, conduct commitments, parity obligations imposed as remedy?

6. **Interaction with sector regulation.** Does it interact with sector regulation in a way that changes our compliance burden — DMA obligations layered onto Article 102, telecoms access regulation, payment-services regulation, FDI conditionality?

## Policy and doctrinal questions

7. **Policy/doctrinal conversation shift?** Does this signal a shift in policy or doctrinal conversation on dominance, abuse theory, or remedies — open Commission consultation, OECD competition committee working paper, academic preprint advancing a new theory of harm (killer ecosystem effects, intermediation power, exploitative T&Cs in digital markets, conditional pricing), or senior-agency speech telegraphing a position — that we should engage with before it crystallises in a decision?

8. **Economic-theory engagement.** Is the agency or court applying a different economic model than the one we assume? Particularly: how is consumer harm being measured (price, output, quality, innovation, choice), how is the counterfactual constructed, is intent being used as evidence of effect or as a substitute for effect?

## Source and confidence calibration

9. **Source weighting.** Commission Article 102 decisions and CJEU judgments are primary. AG opinions are directional and cap `confidence: medium`. National authority Article 102-equivalent decisions are primary for the relevant national market. Agency speeches and working papers cap at `confidence: medium`. Academic preprints on dominance and abuse theory count as primary signal for Monitor 02.

10. **Worst / best / most-likely reading.** Mandatory before filing. Especially important in abuse cases where the same factual pattern can support several theories of harm.

## Materiality test (gate)

If the answer to all of questions 1–8 is "no, this does not connect to a market where Prosus or an OpCo is plausibly dominant, or to a practice/forum/obligation listed in `/abuse-of-dominance/needs.md` within a 24-month horizon" — **discard, do not file**. Watching-brief precedent reading (for non-dominant markets where the theory of harm is still being developed) is filed at `severity: 2` only.
