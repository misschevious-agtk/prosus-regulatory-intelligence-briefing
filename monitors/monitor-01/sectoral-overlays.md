# Sectoral Overlays

> Prosus does not have one sectoral overlay; it has most of them, distributed across the portfolio. **Sectoral overlays layer on top of the horizontal regimes** (GDPR, EU AI Act, copyright law, etc.). Each one creates its own regulator, its own rulebook, its own enforcement track.
>
> A single Monitor 01 item can land on multiple sectors at once — an EU AI Act high-risk classification for credit scoring lands on **financial services AND AI horizontal AND consumer credit AND employment** (when used for HR). The persona has to know which Prosus operating company a sectoral story attaches to in order to rank it properly.

---

## Financial services — payments, e-money, fintech

**Heavily applicable across the group.**

- **PayU** operates payment processing globally; regulated entities in multiple jurisdictions. **PayU Payments Pvt Ltd in India** under RBI. **PayU GPO** historically across Latam and Africa (partially sold to Rapyd March 2025). **PayU Turkey, PayU Romania, PayU Czech Republic.**
- **iyzico** — Turkish payment institution licensed by **BDDK**.
- **Remitly** (~12% stake) — US-licensed money transmitter regulated by FinCEN and state regulators.
- **BillDesk** in India (PayU's earlier intended-acquisition history).

**Regulatory overlays to track:**
- EU **PSD2/PSD3** and forthcoming Payment Services Regulation; **EBA guidelines** on payments and fraud.
- EU **MiCA** where crypto rails come into scope.
- **UK FCA** Payment Services Regulations.
- **Brazil's Pix and BCB** oversight of payment institutions.
- **India's RBI** directions on payment systems, payment aggregators, prepaid instruments.
- **Turkey's BDDK** regime.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** financial-services AI guidance (**EBA AI principles, RBI's draft AI guidance for the financial sector, US OCC/FRB/FDIC interagency AI statements, MAS FEAT principles**) becomes **Rank A automatically** when it addresses model risk, fraud detection, customer-facing chatbots, or KYC/AML automation — PayU and iyzico are squarely in scope.

## Consumer credit and lending

**Live and material.**

- **PayU India** operates as an NBFC under an RBI licence. **LazyPay and PaySense** (merged into a full-stack consumer lending platform).
- **Mintifi** (Prosus India venture) — supply-chain lender.
- **Remitly stake** touches consumer cross-border remittance.

> The Indian RBI has been **the most aggressive consumer-credit regulator in any Prosus jurisdiction over the past three years** — digital lending guidelines, FLDG rules, default-loss-guarantee restrictions, prohibition on first-loss arrangements, recent push on AI-driven underwriting transparency.

**Overlays:**
- RBI Digital Lending Guidelines and circulars.
- **CFPB** rules in the US for any cross-border touch.
- **EU Consumer Credit Directive 2 (CCD2)** — applicable from **November 2026**.
- **UK FCA CONC** rules.
- Brazil's BCB consumer credit and open finance regulations.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** any **AI-in-credit-scoring** development is **Rank A**. The EU AI Act high-risk list includes creditworthiness assessment, which directly catches PayU's lending tech. ADM provisions under **GDPR Article 22** and **LGPD Article 20** bite specifically here.

## Health and pharmaceuticals

**Applicable in two distinct ways.**

- **PharmEasy** — e-pharmacy, prescription handling, diagnostic tests, teleconsultations, health records. One of India's largest online pharmacies; **processes prescription data at scale**.
- **iFood's pharma delivery line** — search for pharma on iFood increased orders 4.6% per Prosus's own LCM disclosures, meaning iFood is processing health-adjacent data and recommendations.
- **Swiggy's Instamart pharma partnership with PharmEasy** adds exposure on the Swiggy stake side.

**Overlays:**
- India's **Drugs and Cosmetics Act 1940** and **draft e-pharmacy rules** (unfinalised; active scrutiny from DCGI and AIOCD).
- **EU MDR/IVDR** if any diagnostic or wellness tooling becomes regulated.
- **HIPAA** in any US touchpoint.
- Sensitive-data provisions under **GDPR Article 9, LGPD Article 11, DPDP Act** special categories.

> **AI Act high-risk list also captures medical-device-adjacent AI**, which means iFood's recommender, if it ranks pharma products, **sits closer to the high-risk line than its food recommender does**.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** any health-data, e-pharmacy, or medical-AI development reaching India, Brazil, or the EU is **Rank A**.

## Children and minors

**Materially applicable in two distinct ways.**

### Marketplace exposure
iFood, Despegar, OLX, eMAG, JET, Swiggy all serve users who may be minors and host products/services targeting families.

### Edtech
**Stack Overflow** (developer community, age varies), **GoodHabitz** (corporate training), plus minority stakes in **Brainly, Udemy, GoStudent, Eruditus, BYJU's, Platzi, Codecademy, SoloLearn** — several explicitly serve K–12 and tutoring markets. **Brainly in particular reaches K–12 students at very large scale.** Skillsoft is enterprise but parts of the Udemy/Codecademy book overlap with under-18 users.

**Overlays:**
- **COPPA** in the US.
- **UK Children's Code** (Age Appropriate Design Code) and Online Safety Act provisions for child safety.
- **EU DSA's minor-protection provisions** and the forthcoming child-safety code under the AI Act.
- **California Age Appropriate Design Code Act** and similar US state laws.
- LGPD's specific provisions on minors in Brazil.
- India's DPDP Act provisions on children (parental consent; prohibition on tracking children).
- **Council of Europe Convention 108+** minor protections.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** anything addressing **minors and AI, recommender systems for minors, advertising to minors, biometric/identity verification of minors** is **Rank A**. AI persona should also flag any **age-assurance technology** development given the rolling regulatory push in UK, EU, and US states.

## Employment and HR / automated decision-making in employment

**Applicable in two forms.**

### Deployer-side
Every Prosus operating company is itself an employer using AI in hiring, performance, and workforce decisions. **Toqan is deployed across 15,000+ employees and 24 portfolio companies**, putting it squarely in scope for any "AI in workplace" rule.

### Platform-side (more consequential)
**iFood, JET, Swiggy, and SkipTheDishes are gig-economy platforms managing couriers via algorithmic systems** — dispatch, pricing, ratings, deactivation. This is exactly the population the **EU Platform Work Directive (transposition deadline December 2026)** targets — including provisions on algorithmic management, transparency, and human oversight of consequential automated decisions about platform workers.

**Sectoral instruments:**
- **EU Platform Work Directive.**
- **Illinois HB 3773 and NYC AEDT Local Law 144** — automated employment decision tools.
- **Colorado AI Act** (lists employment as high-risk).
- **EU AI Act Annex III** employment-and-workers provisions.
- Brazilian labour court rulings on gig workers (particularly live area).
- **UK Employment Rights Bill** provisions on algorithmic management.
- **ICO guidance on AI in recruitment.**

**Monitor 01 ranking:** AI-in-employment and platform-work algorithmic management developments are **Rank A** because they hit the gig platforms operationally, not just the HR function.

## Online platforms / online intermediaries / VLOPs

**Applicable as a sectoral overlay in the EU (DSA, DMA) and the UK (Online Safety Act).**

> None of the Prosus operating companies has been designated a **DMA gatekeeper**, but OLX Group, JET, and eMAG sit comfortably above the **DSA thresholds for online platforms** and may approach VLOP status in some member states.

**Direct address:** DSA's transparency, illegal-content, recommender-system, and risk-assessment obligations apply directly. **Trusted flagger and notice-and-action provisions land on the marketplace operating companies.**

**Sectoral instruments:**
- DSA, DMA (track even if not designated — gatekeeper thresholds shift).
- **UK Online Safety Act** and **Ofcom codes of practice**.
- **India's IT Rules 2021/2023** amendments on intermediaries and significant social media intermediaries.
- **Brazil's Marco Civil** and proposed **PL 2630** ("Fake News bill"); platform-accountability rules emerging from **STF jurisprudence**.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** DSA enforcement actions, recommender-system transparency rulings, Ofcom codes — **Rank A** (operating companies are direct addressees).

## Critical infrastructure / NIS2 and cybersecurity

**Applicable through specific operating companies.**

> EU **NIS2 Directive** (transposition deadline October 2024, **now in enforcement phase**) catches "essential" and "important" entities across multiple sectors including digital infrastructure, online marketplaces above thresholds, and certain payment service providers.

- **PayU's payment processing entities** are squarely in scope.
- **JET, OLX, eMAG** as large online marketplaces are likely "important entities" under NIS2 in their host member states.

**Other regimes:**
- UK equivalents under the **NIS Regulations 2018** (being revised).
- India's **CERT-In** rules and forthcoming Digital India Act cyber provisions.
- Brazil's LGPD plus the forthcoming cybersecurity framework via ANPD/GSI.

**Monitor 01 ranking:** NIS2 enforcement actions, supervisory authority guidance, incident-reporting precedent — **Rank A** for the affected operating companies.

## Ranking rules — sectoral overlays

Two operator-specified rules added to `ranking-criteria.md`:

### Rule 7 — sectoral-overlay upgrade

When a Monitor 01 trigger touches one of the sectoral overlays above AND the affected Prosus operating company is in that sector, **the rank moves up by one tier from the default**.

> Example: a generic CNIL guidance document on AI hits Rank B normally, but if it specifically addresses **AI-in-payments**, it becomes **Rank A for PayU/iyzico**.

This prevents the ranker from treating "AI in credit scoring" the same as "AI in retail," which it shouldn't.

### Rule 8 — multi-sector items

When a single trigger lands on two or more sectoral overlays at once (very common for AI rules — e.g. EU AI Act high-risk classifications hit financial services, employment, and health simultaneously), **the rank is set by the highest-affected sector**, and the finding records all affected sectors in the `secondary_axes` field of the schema.

This is the multi-domain analogue of the existing cross-domain filing rule.

## Living document

The sectoral exposures will move with Prosus's stated strategy of **$2bn+ asset disposals in 2026** and further sales in 2027.

- **PharmEasy, Mintifi, several edtech stakes**, and the smaller venture book are **candidate disposal categories** that would remove specific sectoral overlays from `our_markets`.
- Conversely, **any move into digital banking** (the BillDesk-era language signalled; PayU's NBFC posture keeps it live) would bring **full banking-regulation overlays** online.

**Treat the sectoral map as a living MD reviewed quarterly alongside the markets and stack maps.**
