## N1 — Categories of news that trigger a finding

- ECN, DG COMP, and Tier A NCA dawn raids — sectoral, jurisdictional, and any naming pattern.
- Statements of objections, infringement decisions, settlements, and commitment decisions under Article 101 TFEU and national-law equivalents in Tier A jurisdictions.
- Court rulings under Article 101 — CJEU (Grand Chamber and Chambers), General Court, UK CAT and Court of Appeal, US federal courts on Sherman §1, Brazilian STF/STJ on horizontal-restraint doctrine, Indian CCI Appeal Board, Indian NCLAT.
- MFN/parity-clause decisions, settlements, and court rulings — particularly Booking.com/Hotelreservierung line of authority, food-delivery price-parity cases, hotel and travel platform cases, retail platform fairness/parity cases.
- Information-exchange enforcement actions — AC-Treuhand, Bananas, T-Mobile Netherlands progeny; trade-association cases; competitor-data-sharing enforcement.
- Algorithmic pricing and tacit-collusion enforcement actions, market studies (CMA, CADE, BKartA, ACM, DOJ Antitrust Division), and academic literature on algorithmic alignment.
- Hub-and-spoke decisions and the developing common-intermediary doctrine.
- Vertical restraints under VBER 2022 and national-equivalent block exemptions — RPM, exclusivity, online sales restrictions, selective distribution, platform bans, Coty line progeny.
- iFood/CADE TCC monitoring developments, modification proposals, alleged-breach signals, and the CADE's broader food-delivery enforcement posture in Brazil.
- Leniency programme amendments, leniency-award decisions, leniency-plus developments in Tier A jurisdictions.
- Cartel damages litigation developments and the Skanska / Sumal progeny on parental liability.
- ECN-level cooperation patterns and joint-NCA actions.
- Cross-border cartel coordination — particularly EU-US-UK-Brazil patterns.
- Trade-association case enforcement — sectoral focus on food-delivery, classifieds, payments, travel, e-tail.
- Academic and policy literature on horizontal-restraint doctrine, vertical-restraint doctrine, and algorithmic-pricing-as-collusion — TILEC, Toulouse, CRESSE, CLEEN, Stigler Center work; SSRN antitrust working papers.
- OECD competition committee work on horizontal restraints, vertical restraints, and digital cartels.
- Insurance market signals on cartel/leniency risk — D&O policies, antitrust-specific cover changes.

## N2 — Out of scope

- Pure Article 102 / Sherman §2 abuse-of-dominance developments without a horizontal-restraint angle → `abuse-of-dominance` reading-mode.
- Pure merger control without coordination theories of harm → `merger-control-fdi` reading-mode.
- Pure DMA/DSA enforcement without Article 101 / horizontal-restraint angle → `digital-markets-sector-state-aid` reading-mode.
- Pure consumer protection without competition-law anchor.
- US-state-AG antitrust litigation in jurisdictions outside `our_markets` and without precedent value.
- General "competition law is broken" commentary without doctrinal anchor.
