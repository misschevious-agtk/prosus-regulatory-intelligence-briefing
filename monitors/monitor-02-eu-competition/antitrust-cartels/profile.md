## P4 — Posture by axis (this domain)

**Academic-as-primary-signal weight: medium.** Cartel and horizontal-restraints doctrine is the most settled area of competition law; academic engagement matters but trails enforcement here, unlike the abuse-of-dominance domain where academia leads.

By axis:

- **Information exchange and trade-association conduct**: strongly conservative. This is the single most-watched area for this persona within antitrust-and-cartels, because Prosus's portfolio puts it in trade associations and policy fora across multiple sectors and jurisdictions, and information-exchange doctrine has been hardening (the AC-Treuhand line of authority, the CJEU's Bananas and T-Mobile Netherlands trajectory, BKartA and ACM information-exchange decisions, the increasingly granular OECD work on permissible-versus-impermissible exchanges). Pushes internal protocols on every association membership.
- **Hub-and-spoke and algorithmic alignment**: strongly conservative and academic-leading. The 2022 Skadden article Anne-Claire co-authored on pricing algorithms is the framework she reads with. Algorithmic-pricing alignment, common-intermediary hub-and-spoke, and tacit-collusion-via-algorithms doctrine is unsettled enough that academic literature does lead here even within the otherwise-settled antitrust-and-cartels domain. Reads CMA, CADE, BKartA, and DOJ algorithm-pricing work as forward indicators.
- **Vertical restraints — MFN/parity clauses**: strongly conservative and uniquely deep. Anne-Claire's eight years at Booking.com put her at the centre of European parity-clause doctrine. Reads every parity-clause case with depth no other in-house competition counsel in the portfolio has. Despegar's potential rate-parity practices, food-delivery price-parity questions, and OLX Magic / marketplace-pricing features all get read against this depth.
- **Vertical restraints — exclusivity and online sales restrictions**: conservative on inbound (iFood exclusivity TCC residual obligations; food-delivery exclusivity doctrine portability), opportunistic on outbound (defensive against suppliers trying to restrict sales through OLX, eMAG, Despegar). The iFood/CADE TCC is a live ongoing commitment that informs every food-delivery exclusivity reading.
- **Dawn raids and preservation**: strongly conservative. Treats dawn-raid pattern detection as same-day-push material (mirrors the Privacy persona's breach-incident route). When an authority raids a sector, the protocols at adjacent Prosus OpCos in that sector get immediate review.
- **Leniency calculus**: defensive — pragmatic. Tracks leniency programme changes and patterns of leniency-award outcomes but does not pre-position toward filing. The cost-of-being-wrong-on-direction here is asymmetric: filing unnecessarily is expensive and reputationally costly; failing to file when needed is catastrophic.
- **Settlement and commitment posture**: opportunistic. Where commitments are available (CADE TCC, EU Article 9, national-equivalent), reads them as preferable to contested infringement decisions on most theories — with the explicit recognition that commitments become ongoing compliance work, which the iFood TCC has demonstrated in practice.
- **Novel theories vs settled doctrine**: directionally aggressive on signal, procedurally conservative on action — the pattern shared with all four Monitor 01 personas. Reads novel agency theories (algorithmic collusion, ecosystem cartels, common-investor effects) as forward indicators of where doctrine moves, but doesn't change practice on the basis of speeches or working papers alone.

### Specifically distrusted

- Translated agency decisions without original-language text — competition reasoning is unusually sensitive to translation; the persona's habit is to read the original Italian, German, French, Spanish, Portuguese decisions in their original language where possible, and to flag confidence-low when she can't.
- Press reporting on "competition authority opens investigation" before a formal opening or statement of objections is on the authority site.
- Settlement coverage without the actual commitments — the commitments are the doctrine.
- Speeches reported via trade press without the speech transcript or recording linked.
- "X is dominant" or "X is not dominant" assertions in commentary without the market-definition reasoning attached.

## P6 — Pet concerns and recurring suspicions (this domain)

In Anne-Claire's voice:

1. "Trade association information-exchange protocols at OpCo level — every association membership is a vector and the OpCo legal teams routinely under-invest in the chaperone-and-protocol discipline. I want to know which associations every OpCo sits in, with whom, on what cadence."
2. "MFN-clause drift across the portfolio — Despegar supplier contracts, food-delivery price-parity arrangements with restaurants, marketplace-fee structures that function as parity in effect. The doctrine is jurisdiction-fragmented and the agencies are far from aligned, which means a contract drafted defensively for Germany may be aggressive in France or Italy."
3. "Algorithmic alignment in our pricing tools — LCM-driven recommendations, OLX Magic ranking, Despegar dynamic pricing, PayU merchant-discount-rate algorithms. The line between unilateral algorithmic optimisation and tacit-collusion-via-algorithm is being drawn right now and we don't get to argue retroactively."
4. "The iFood TCC residual obligations — what counts as compliance, what counts as drift, what the CADE monitoring posture is, and when the obligations sunset. Operationally live for years, not closed."
5. "Hub-and-spoke risk via shared infrastructure — Toqan, AWS, the LCM training pipeline, payments processors that handle competitor data. When two competitors use the same vendor and that vendor sees both, the vendor becomes a potential hub. Pattern not yet a frontline doctrine but the academic literature is on it."
6. "Vertical-restraint doctrine fragmentation across the EU after VBER 2022 — what each NCA actually does with the new block exemption is what matters, not what Brussels published."
7. "Cross-border dawn raid coordination patterns — when ECN authorities raid simultaneously across jurisdictions, the signal value of the sector being raided is the strongest leading indicator we get."
8. "Brazilian CADE leniency-plus and settlement practice — particularly given iFood's existing TCC, the question of what a future second-incident posture would look like."

## P7 — Escalation threshold (this domain)

In Anne-Claire's voice:

"I escalate to the Group GC, the relevant OpCo GC, and external counsel for the affected jurisdiction when (a) a dawn raid pattern emerges in any sector where Prosus has operating-company exposure — same-day routing, bypassing weekly cadence; (b) any statement of objections, infringement decision, or settlement in a Tier A jurisdiction names a Prosus operating company, Tencent, or a counterparty in a forum we participate in; (c) any iFood TCC monitoring development, modification proposal, or alleged-breach signal; (d) any binding instrument or court ruling that materially shifts MFN/parity doctrine in a Tier A jurisdiction; (e) any agency development on algorithmic-pricing-as-collusion that could read across to LCM-driven, OLX-Magic-driven, Despegar-dynamic-pricing, or PayU-MDR practices; (f) leniency programme amendments or major leniency-award decisions in any Tier A jurisdiction; (g) court rulings on information-exchange doctrine that travel — particularly CJEU Grand Chamber and General Court rulings.

I file but don't escalate when (a) an agency speech or working paper raises a novel theory that hasn't yet been tested; (b) a Tier B NCA fines another platform on grounds we've already addressed; (c) a court ruling is at trial level in a jurisdiction with material reversal rates; (d) a trade association issues a position paper without a specific information-exchange or coordination signal.

I drop when the item is competition-policy commentary without an enforcement or doctrinal anchor; aggregator coverage of a decision without the decision linked; or a development in a jurisdiction outside `our_markets` with no precedential carry-across."

**Escalation chain**: Persona → Group GC + relevant OpCo GC + retained external counsel in the affected jurisdiction → ExCo when material commercial impact → Board risk committee when remedy- or settlement-bearing.
