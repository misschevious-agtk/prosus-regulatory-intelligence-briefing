# Interrogation Checklist — Merger Control & Foreign Investment Screening

Run every incoming item against this checklist before deciding to escalate to a finding. The checklist is stable across deployments; the `needs.md` for this domain is what determines whether a "yes" answer is *material* to Prosus.

A standing context note: Prosus is both acquisitive and disposing at material scale (Despegar 2025; JET 2025; OLX/La Centrale; PayU GPO partial sale; $2bn+ disposal target for 2026 with continuation in 2027). The Delivery Hero divestment commitment from the August 2025 JET EU clearance is an active EUMR remedy obligation. The iFood TCC from CADE 2023 is an active behavioural commitment. Both belong inside the materiality lens of every merger-control item, not just deal-specific items.

## Enforcement-and-effects questions

1. **Affects a deal we are doing, considering, or could be a target of?** Does this affect a current deal, a deal under consideration, the Delivery Hero divestment commitment, the iFood TCC, or position us as a plausible target? Acquirer-side, target-side, and counterparty-side analysis all in scope.

2. **Jurisdictional thresholds, call-in powers, referral mechanisms.** Does it change the jurisdictional thresholds, call-in powers, or referral mechanisms — Article 22 EUMR referrals (Illumina/Grail line and successors), national below-threshold call-in tools (CMA discretion, German GWB call-in, Italian below-threshold, Austrian transaction-value threshold, Danish call-in, Swedish call-in, Hungarian call-in), or new mechanisms emerging in our markets?

3. **Substantive test shift in our markets.** Does it shift the substantive test in our markets — SIEC, SLC, dominance test, or theories of harm including killer acquisitions, ecosystem theories, conglomerate effects, innovation theories, data-aggregation theories, vertical-foreclosure theories, two-sided-platform theories, gatekeeper-of-the-future theories?

4. **Remedy practice shift.** Does it change remedy practice — upfront-buyer requirements, fix-it-first, behavioural-remedies acceptance (the Commission has been moving on this; CMA divergence), divestiture timelines, monitoring-trustee practice, hold-separate management, parity obligations as remedy, access remedies, interoperability remedies?

5. **FDI / FSR scope.** Does it touch FDI screening or the Foreign Subsidies Regulation — new sectors in scope, new thresholds, new prohibitions, new conditions, new mitigation patterns, sector designations under national FDI regimes, FSR notification thresholds, ex-officio FSR investigations? UK NSI Act activity also tracked here.

6. **Gun-jumping enforcement.** Does it signal a gun-jumping enforcement pattern we should reflect in our deal protocols — clean-team practice, information exchange in due diligence, integration planning before clearance, premature operational coordination, signalling-as-implementation theories?

## Policy and doctrinal questions

7. **Policy/doctrinal conversation shift?** Does this signal a shift in policy or doctrinal conversation on jurisdictional reach, theories of harm in mergers, remedy posture, or FDI/FSR scope — open consultation, working paper, agency speech, OECD competition committee output — that we should engage with before it lands in revised guidance or a precedent decision? Particularly relevant: the post-*Illumina/Grail* CJEU judgment landscape on Article 22 and where DG COMP and NCAs are repositioning.

8. **Economic-theory engagement.** What is the economic theory of harm being applied? Killer-acquisition theories rely on contestable assumptions about pipeline products; ecosystem theories rely on contestable definitions of complementarity; innovation theories rely on contestable counterfactuals. Where is the agency on the theory-application curve, and is the counterfactual being tested or assumed?

## Source and confidence calibration

9. **Source weighting.** Clearance decisions, prohibitions, Phase II openings, court judgments on appeal, formal guidance — all primary. Agency speeches and informal guidance directional only, capped at `confidence: medium`. Academic literature on killer acquisitions, ecosystem theories, and below-threshold tools is primary signal for Monitor 02.

10. **Worst / best / most-likely reading.** Mandatory before filing. Especially important on call-in and below-threshold-tool items, where the worst reading is often "this expands jurisdictional reach in our markets in ways not signalled by the text."

## Materiality test (gate)

If the answer to all of questions 1–8 is "no, this does not connect to a deal, an ongoing remedy commitment, a jurisdictional change, or an FDI/FSR scope change affecting our pipeline or our existing transactions within a 24-month horizon" — **discard, do not file**. Below-threshold call-in patterns in our sectors are not discarded even where no current deal is affected, because the deal pipeline horizon extends past the immediate.
