## Summary excerpt

Brussels, 25 June - The EDPB has published an update of the One-Stop-Shop (OSS) case digest on right to object and right to erasure . This project has been developed in the framework of the of the Support Pool of Experts programme, which aims to support cooperation among Data Protection Authorities (DPAs). Thematic one-stop-shop case digests are drafted on the basis of one-stop-shop decisions taken from the EDPB’s public register (based on Art.60 GDPR). Such case digests complement the EDPB's public register by selecting and presenting the most important decisions on a given theme and providing aggregate results of relevant decisions on this theme. The one-stop-shop thematic case digest on the right to object and right to erasure offers insights into how DPAs analyse the internal processes implemented within organisations to comply with these rights . It also lists the most frequent infringements and gives an overview of which corrective measures have been issued . Cases cover for example the exercise of the right to object to direct marketing or the wish of individuals to erase their account or online data profile.
